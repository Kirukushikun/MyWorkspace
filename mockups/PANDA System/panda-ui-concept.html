Help

Glossary — Statuses, Tags & Roles

What each status means, who can act on it, and where the PAN goes next. The pills and colors here are the same ones used across the system.

The full journey of a PAN
Draft→ Division Head→ Tag & HR Preparation→ DH Confirmation→ HR Approval→ Final Approval→ Served→ Filed

Any stage can also branch backwards: the Division Head returns to the Requestor, the HR Approver returns one step to the Preparer, and the Final Approver rejects all the way back to HR Preparation. Once filed, a follow-up PAN can start a new cycle for the same employee.

Statuses
StatusWhat it meansWho can actWhat can happen next
Draft Saved by the Requestor but not yet submitted. Attachment optional at this point. Requestor Submit to Division Head · edit · delete outright
With Division Head Formally submitted (PDF required) and awaiting the department head's decision. Read-only to the Requestor. Division Head (DH Head if Manila) Approved → HR Preparation · returned to Requestor with a reason
Returned — for correction Sent back to the Requestor with a preset or custom reason (e.g. incomplete document). Requestor Replace attachment & resubmit · withdraw entirely
Awaiting tag & preparation Division-approved, but paperwork can't start until a confidentiality tag (Tarlac or Manila) is applied. Any HR Preparer Tagged Tarlac → normal preparer works it · tagged Manila → HR Head only
HR Preparation The official paperwork is being filled in: employment details and the Action Reference From/To table. HR Preparer (HR Head if Manila) Submitted for DH confirmation · voided with a reason
For DH Confirmation HR has prepared the PAN; the Division Head reviews what was actually drafted. Division Head (DH Head if Manila) Confirmed → HR Approval · disputed → back to the Preparer
Returned by HR Approver Sent one step back to HR Preparation for rework, with a mandatory reason. HR Preparer Revised & resubmitted · voided with a reason
For HR Approval Second HR-level check after preparation and confirmation. No confidentiality distinction at this stage. HR Approver Approved → Final Approval · returned one step to the Preparer
For Final Approval Awaiting the last sign-off — individually or in bulk (by selection or by action type). Final Approver Approved (Regularization auto-sets status to "Regular") · rejected → all the way back to HR Preparation
Approved — for serving Fully approved; the action now has to be carried out in the real world. HR Preparer Marked Served · marked Unserved with a reason
Served The action has been carried out and acknowledged. HR Preparer Marked Filed to close the record
Unserved Approved but couldn't be carried out — AWOL, resigned, terminated, or a custom reason. — Terminal — kept on record
Filed Closed out and archived. The PAN's "To" values become the starting "From" values of the employee's next PAN. HR Preparer Start a follow-up PAN for the same employee
Withdrawn The Requestor pulled back a request that had been returned to them, instead of resubmitting. — Terminal — kept on record
Voided Deleted by HR while awaiting preparation or resolution, with a mandatory reason on record. — Terminal — reason visible in the Audit Trail

Confidentiality tags

  Manila — highly confidential

Prepared only by an HR Head Preparer; a normal preparer who opens or tags it is locked out permanently. At the division stages, only the DH Head — not the department's regular Division Head — can see or act on it.

  Tarlac — routine

The normal path. Worked by ordinary HR Preparers; visible to the department's Division Head as usual.

  Untagged

Freshly approved by the Division Head. Preparation stays locked until any HR Preparer applies one of the two tags. Tag dots are visible to HR Head Preparers only.

Roles

RequestorRaises PANs for their department(s)
Division HeadApproves/returns, then confirms prepared PANs
DH HeadDivision-stage decisions on Manila PANs, all departments
HR PreparerTags and fills in the official paperwork
HR Head PreparerSame, plus exclusive handling of Manila PANs
HR ApproverSecond check before final sign-off
Final ApproverLast approval, single or bulk
AdminUsers, access, employees, logs, maintenance

One person can hold any combination of these — e.g. Requestor + Division Head. Department assignments for requesting and heading are independent, and a department can have several co-heads.
